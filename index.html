REFERENCE & FAQ
Getting Started
Safe materials: Acrylic (cast & extruded), wood/plywood/MDF (up to 12mm), leather, cardstock/paper, rubber, cork, fabric, and anodized metals (marking only). ⚠ NEVER cut: PVC, vinyl containing chlorine, polycarbonate (Lexan), ABS plastic, fiberglass, or any material containing halogens — they release toxic/corrosive gases that harm you and the machine.
1. Turn on the exhaust fan — wait 5 seconds. 2. Turn on the chiller unit — wait for it to reach target temp (18–20°C). 3. Flip the main power switch on the laser. 4. Turn the key to ON. 5. Home the machine using the controller panel. 6. Launch LightBurn on your connected computer.
The Boss HP 2440 uses a manual focus tool (small acrylic block included). Place the block on your material surface, then jog Z down until the laser head just touches the top of the block. Remove the block before firing. Tip: measure material thickness in mm and note it in your job for future reference.
Troubleshooting
• Re-focus on the material surface • Increase power (+5–10%) • Decrease speed (–5 mm/s) • Add an extra pass • Clean the lens with isopropyl alcohol + lens tissue • Check air assist nozzle isn't blocked • Make sure material is flat — use hold-down pins or tape
• Clean all mirrors and the focusing lens • Check belt tension — loose belts cause banding • Reduce scan speed • Tape material down so it doesn't move • In LightBurn: enable 'Overscan' to avoid edge fade • Try 254 or 300 DPI as a starting point
• Check limit switches are clean and clear of debris • Slow down homing speed in LightBurn Machine Settings • Check for debris on rails preventing full travel • Re-run homing cycle from the controller panel
• Verify the lens is seated correctly in the head • Check mirror alignment — a misaligned beam won't hit the lens • Confirm lid interlock is triggering correctly • Ensure power is above minimum threshold (~12% for CO2 tubes)
Safety
• NEVER leave the machine unattended while running • Keep a CO2 fire extinguisher within arm's reach at all times • Always run exhaust before and after cutting • Foam, thick wood, and rubber have high fire risk • Keep a spray bottle of water nearby for flare-ups • If fire: hit E-STOP, smother or extinguish — do NOT open lid immediately (feeds oxygen)
• Exhaust must run before the first pulse of the laser • Vent outside or through HEPA/activated-carbon filter • When cutting acrylic, run exhaust 2–3 minutes after job ends • If you smell strong burning/chemical odors, stop and check exhaust flow

LIGHTBURN + BOSS HP 2440 GUIDE

Complete setup and operation guide for LightBurn with the Boss HP 2440 laser cutter.

Bed Size
24" × 40"
609.6 × 1016 mm
Controller
Ruida RDC6445G
DSP License required
Tube Power
60W CO2
Max 80% for tube longevity
Origin
Top-Left (Rear)
Set in LightBurn Device Settings
1. Get the right license
The Boss HP 2440 uses a Ruida RDC6445G controller. You need LightBurn's DSP license (not GRBL). Download from lightburnsoftware.com. A trial period is available.
2. Add your device
Open LightBurn → click "Devices" (bottom right) → "Find My Laser" or "Create Manually". Select "Ruida" as controller type. Choose USB. Set bed size to 24" × 40" (609.6 × 1016 mm).
3. Set origin
Boss HP 2440 homes to the rear-left corner (top-left in LightBurn's view). In Device Settings, set "Origin" to Top-Left. Test by jogging — the head should move in the expected direction.
4. Install Ruida drivers
If LightBurn doesn't see the machine via USB, install the Ruida USB driver from the Boss Laser support page. On Windows it appears as a COM port in Device Manager.
5. Test connection
With machine powered and USB connected, click the Move arrows in LightBurn. The laser head should move. If not, check USB cable and confirm the machine is fully booted and homed.
Workspace / Canvas
The large center area represents your 24"×40" bed. Import files by dragging SVG, DXF, PNG, JPG, or PDF directly onto the canvas. The dashed rectangle is your machine boundary — keep designs inside it.
Cuts / Layers panel
Each color layer = a different operation. Click a layer to set Power, Speed, Mode (Cut / Scan / Scan+Cut), Passes, and Air Assist. Drag layers to reorder — typically engrave first, then cut.
Laser panel (bottom right)
Contains Start, Pause, Stop, and Frame buttons. "Frame" runs the head around your design's bounding box without firing — always frame before cutting to confirm placement.
Console tab
Shows real-time communication with the controller. Useful for diagnosing connection issues. Repeated errors usually mean a USB or power issue.
Move / Position tab
Lets you jog the laser head, set job start position, and return to origin. "Set Laser Position" and "Get Position" are useful for precise material placement.
1. Import your file
File → Import (or drag and drop). SVG and DXF are best for cutting. PNG/JPG work for engraving. PDF works but may need cleanup — verify paths after importing.
2. Assign layers
Select objects and click a color in the bottom palette to assign a layer. Convention: Red = cut outline, Black = raster engrave, Blue = vector engrave. Each layer gets its own power/speed settings.
3. Set layer mode
"Line" for cutting/vector engraving, "Fill" for raster/filled engraving, "Fill+Line" to engrave then cut the outline.
4. Enter power & speed
Start with conservative settings from the material presets tab. Always test on scrap first. Keep max power under 80% for tube longevity.
5. Set passes
For thick materials, increase passes rather than maxing power. Multiple moderate passes give cleaner edges and less char than one high-power blast.
6. Frame the design
Click "Frame" to preview the bounding box — the head traces the outline without firing. Confirm the design fits your material before cutting.
7. Send to laser
"Start" sends and begins immediately. "Send" uploads to controller memory so you can start from the machine panel — useful if you want to step away from the computer first.
Material Test Generator
Edit → Material Test — creates a grid of test squares at varying power and speed. Run on scrap to find optimal settings. Save good results as a named Material Preset.
Saving Material Presets
In the Cuts panel, right-click a layer → "Save as Preset". Name it by material and thickness (e.g. "Acrylic 3mm Cut"). Presets load instantly for future jobs.
Kerf Offset
For precise press-fit parts: in layer settings enable "Kerf Offset" and enter half your measured kerf width. Typical CO2 kerf on acrylic: 0.1–0.15mm, so offset = 0.075mm.
Tabs / Bridges
Prevent small pieces from falling: enable "Tabs" in layer settings. Set tab size (1–2mm) and count. The laser leaves small uncut bridges to hold pieces in place.
Rotary Setup
Laser → Rotary Setup. Enter steps/rotation and object diameter. Always test with low power on a dummy object first.
Camera Overlay (optional)
Boss sells a compatible LightBurn camera. Once calibrated, you see a live overhead image overlaid on your canvas — click exactly where on the material you want to place the design.
Controller: Ruida RDC6445G
Fully supported by LightBurn DSP license. Check for Ruida firmware updates from Boss Laser support when upgrading LightBurn for best compatibility.
Tube: 60W CO2
Minimum firing power: ~12–15%. Keep under 80% for tube longevity. Do not run sustained jobs at 100% power. Typical tube lifespan: 1,000–2,000 hours.
Chiller: CW-5000 / CW-3000
Set target water temp to 20°C (68°F). Never run the laser if the chiller alarm is active. Consider adding a startup delay in LightBurn Machine Settings to let the chiller reach temp first.
Air Assist
Enable "Air Assist" on cut layers (the fan icon in layer settings). Blows air at the cut point to reduce char, clear smoke, and reduce fire risk significantly.
Speed Units
Ruida in LightBurn shows speed in mm/s. Boss Laser docs sometimes use mm/min. Convert: divide mm/min by 60. Example: 1800 mm/min = 30 mm/s.
USB vs. Ethernet
USB is standard. For network access: configure a static IP on the Ruida panel, then add it in LightBurn Device Settings under "TCP/IP". Useful in shared shop environments.
Before every job
□ Exhaust fan is running □ Chiller is ON and at temp (≤ 20°C) □ Lens inspected and clean □ Mirrors clean □ Material is flat and secured to bed □ Focus set for material thickness □ Air assist connected and working □ Fire extinguisher within reach □ Framed design to verify placement □ Operator will stay present for the entire job
